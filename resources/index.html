Resources

Listed below are some of the people I follow who share great content with the community. The list contains both people I know personally and peers I follow in industry, each one of them a source of inspiration in my own development.

Kelvin Charles

  • Blog: networkwizkid.com
  • YouTube: Network Wiizkiid

A longtime personal friend from University and one of the hardest working people I know. Kelvin currently works for Cisco as a Security Consulting Engineer and has a good selection of specialist security content that he updates regularly on his pursuit of the CCIE Security certification.

Laurence Curling

  • Website: Mode44.co.uk
  • YouTube: Mode44

Laurence is a former colleague with many years of network engineering and consultancy experience. Now working in the network security consultancy/contractor space, his blog showcases just a sample of the technical skills he possesses and the services that Mode44 offer.

Sergey Lisitsin

  • YouTube: NetWizz

Another former colleague and Cisco VIP. My first exposure to Ansible was through a demonstration he did when working together. He is a big contributor to the Cisco Community forums and his videos are great for learning Ansible.

John McGovern (IPvZero)

  • Guest post on nornir.tech: Combining Nornir with pyATS
  • YouTube: IPvZero

John’s videos are well made, easy to follow and cover a wide range of networking topics. His Key Concepts and Python Network Automation video series in particular are excellent resources.

Hank Preston

  • Blog: Cisco: Hank Preston
  • Talk: PyCon 2020: Introduction to Writing Network Tests with pyATS
  • Video Course: Cisco Developer: Network Programmability Basics

Long time follower and admirer of Hank’s work in the Network Automation space. Alongside the great content on his blog and the guides he does for Cisco DevNet, Hank also hosts a regular webinar and recently did a talk at PyCon2020 on writing network tests with pyATS.

Nicholas Russo

  • Website: njrusmc.net
  • YouTube: Nicholas Russo

Nick produces some excellent, highly-rated courses on Pluralsight that focus on network automation and NetDevOps. His blog, along with the source code he writes and shares with the community is a great resource and inspiration for my own development.

David Bombal

  • Website: davidbombal.com
  • YouTube: David Bombal

David produces a variety of training materials around CCNA/CCNP exam preparation, Network Automation, Python and much more. Alongside the training he offers through his website, David also produces some excellent videos for free on his YouTube channel.

Julio Gomez

  • Blog: Cisco: Julio Gomez

Julio has written some great NetDevOps content, including a series of guides on using pyATS for network profiling and testing.
